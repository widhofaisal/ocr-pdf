Waktu : 10 hari (take-home/ Due date: 14 Juni 2024)

Instructions:

A. Read the short story entitled Harrison Bergeron by Kurt Vonnegut, the poem: Ozymandias
by Percy Bysshe Shelley, and one-scene drama entitled The Sandbox by Edward Albee
provided in a separate sheet of paper/ link.

B. Answer the following questions:

1. a. How do “Harrison Bergeron,” “Ozymandias,” and “The Sandbox” each depict the
concept of hegemony? Compare and contrast the different forms of power and
control presented in these works.

b. In your own opinion, are there any current situations in the world like or closely
resembling the three works?

2. Consider the historical and social contexts in which Vonnegut, Shelley, and Albee
wrote their works. How do these contexts influence their critiques of hegemony?

3. Examine the role of the setting in each text. How do the dystopian future in “Harrison

Bergeron,” the ancient ruins in “Ozymandias,” and the surreal domestic environment
in “The Sandbox” shape the narrative and the portrayal of hegemony?
